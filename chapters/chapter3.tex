\chapter{Methodology}

\section{Research Design}
This thesis follows a comparative experimental design. The same dataset, preprocessing pipeline, train-validation-test split, and evaluation metrics are used for all models so that the effect of architecture choice and transfer learning can be compared fairly. The study evaluates four main experimental conditions: ResNet50 trained from scratch, ResNet50 with transfer learning, DenseNet121 trained from scratch, and DenseNet121 with transfer learning.

\section{Dataset Description}
The experiments are conducted on the NIH ChestXray14 dataset. This dataset contains chest radiographs annotated with disease labels extracted from radiology reports. In this thesis, the original multi-label annotations are simplified into a binary task. Images labeled ``No Finding'' are treated as the normal class, while images with one or more disease labels are treated as the abnormal class. This mapping creates a clear and manageable classification problem for the capstone.
